Michael 03/07/2023 08:35
I would take the closest look on the most difficult part: the PluginSetupProcessor and its two-step setup process (https://devs.aragon.org/docs/osx/how-it-works/framework/plugin-management/plugin-setup/). First, it allows people to prepare an installation/update/uninstallation and request a list of permission operations (from/to the dao, plugin or other contract or wallet addresses). Second, the DAO calls PluginSetupProcessor for it to apply this list of permission operations. This commonly happens through a governance process (i.e., a proposal is created in, let's say, the TokenVoting plugin, and people have to agree to install/update/uninstall the plugin). The first step gives DAO builders/auditors/tech-savy members time to review/audit the
  • plugin implementation
  • plugin setup contract
  • all other, associated contracts (that receive permissions or have access on the previous two). The security concept is to create certainty/predictability that the permissions that were prepared are exactly the permissions that are applied. To assure this, a lot of hashing takes place in the PluginSetupProcessor (PSP).
If you would find ways / circumstances in which permissions can be applied that differ from those that were prepared / announced, that would certainly be an important finding. (edited)
